Learn how to identify fake job scams and protect yourself from employment fraud

⚠️

Stay Alert — Fake Jobs Are Everywhere!

According to research, over 40% of job ads on WhatsApp and Facebook are suspicious. Use our detection system and follow these safety guidelines to protect yourself.

🚩 Common Red Flags of Fake Jobs
💰

Asking for Money

Any job that asks for a "registration fee", "training fee", or "security deposit" is a scam. Legitimate employers NEVER ask for money before hiring.

💸

Unrealistic Salary

Promises like "Earn Rs. 50,000 daily" or "$5000/week with no experience" are impossible. If it sounds too good to be true — it is!

📱

WhatsApp / Personal Email

Legitimate companies use official email addresses (e.g. hr@company.com). Jobs advertised only via WhatsApp or Gmail are highly suspicious.

⚡

Extreme Urgency

"Apply within 24 hours!" or "Last 2 seats remaining!" are pressure tactics used by scammers to prevent you from thinking carefully.

🏢

Vague Company Information

No company name, no physical address, no phone number — these are major red flags. Always verify the company exists before applying.

📝

No Interview Required

Receiving a job offer without any interview or verification process is highly suspicious. Real companies always interview candidates first.

✅ Safety Tips to Protect Yourself
🔍

Verify Before Applying

Always search for the company on Google, LinkedIn, and official websites before sending your CV or any personal information.

🔒

Never Share Sensitive Info

Do not share your CNIC, bank details, passwords, or OTPs during the application process. No legitimate employer needs these.

💳

Never Pay Anyone

Real jobs pay YOU — you never pay them. Immediately stop communication with anyone asking for money to give you a job.

🌐

Check the Website URL

Use our URL Verification tool to check if a job website is legitimate. Look for HTTPS and check the domain age before trusting it.

👥

Tell Friends & Family

If you find a fake job, warn your contacts. Scammers target multiple people. Sharing awareness can save others from being cheated.

📢

Report Suspicious Jobs

Use the Report Job feature on JobDetect to flag suspicious postings. Your report helps protect the entire community from scammers.
